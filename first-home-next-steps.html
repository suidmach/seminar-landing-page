The Road to Homeownership

1. Get Your Finances in Order
Check your credit, start saving, and open an FHSA if you haven't already.
2. Get Pre-Approved
Know your budget before you shop. Shows sellers you're serious.
3. Find Your Realtor & Start Looking
Partner with a realtor who knows your target areas and start viewing homes.
4. Make an Offer & Go Firm
Submit your offer, finalize your mortgage, and remove conditions.
5. Prepare for Closing
Hire a lawyer, arrange home insurance, and do your final walkthrough.
6. Get Your Keys
Your lawyer registers the home, funds transfer, and you're officially a homeowner.

Pre-Approval Document Checklist

Everyone Needs

  • Government ID Two pieces of government-issued identification
  • Void Cheque For setting up your mortgage payment account
  • Down Payment Proof 90-day transaction history from accounts holding your funds

If You're a Salaried Employee

  • Recent Pay Stubs Two most recent, showing year-to-date earnings
  • Letter of Employment On company letterhead confirming position, salary, and start date
  • T4s & Notice of Assessments Last two years of each from CRA

If You Earn Commission or Bonuses

  • Two-Year Income History T4s and NOAs for the past two years to calculate your average
  • Employer Confirmation Letter confirming your commission/bonus structure

If You're Self-Employed

  • T1 Generals Last two years of complete personal tax returns
  • Notice of Assessments Last two years from CRA
  • Business Financials Financial statements, articles of incorporation, or business licence
  • Proof of Business Activity Recent bank statements or contracts showing ongoing income

If Applicable to Your Situation

  • Gift Letter If family is helping with your down payment, confirming it's a gift
  • Separation or Divorce Documents Finalized agreement showing support obligations and property division
  • Rental Income Documentation If you own other properties: lease agreements and T1 showing rental income

What to Avoid During the Process

  • Don't change jobs â€” lenders want stable employment history
  • Don't take on new debt â€” no new car loans, credit cards, or financing
  • Don't make large deposits â€” unexplained money triggers extra paperwork
  • Don't co-sign for anyone â€” you become responsible for their debt
  • Don't miss any payments â€” even one late payment can affect approval

Down Payment Do's and Don'ts

  • Don't move money around â€” keep your down payment in one account. Every transfer between accounts creates a paper trail you'll need to explain.
  • Keep it low-risk â€” if your down payment is in investments, move it to a savings account or GIC now. Market dips at the wrong time can shrink your buying power.
  • No borrowed deposits â€” don't use a line of credit or loan for your deposit with offer. Lenders will see this as new debt and it can affect your approval.
  • Gift funds? Deposit early â€” if family is gifting part of your down payment, have them transfer it as soon as possible. It needs to be "seasoned" in your account, and you'll need a signed gift letter.
  • Don't open new accounts â€” opening a new savings account to "organize" your down payment just creates more statements to provide and more questions to answer.
  • Large deposits need explanation â€” if you receive any large deposits beyond your regular paycheque, keep records of where they came from. Sold a car? Keep the bill of sale. Tax refund? Keep the notice of assessment.
  • FHSA/RRSP withdrawals need time â€” if you're using First Home Savings Account or RRSP Home Buyers' Plan, start the withdrawal process early. It can take 1-2 weeks to access the funds.
  • Save your statements â€” keep 90 days of bank statements showing your down payment sitting untouched. You'll need these for your application.
